Cap weekly LLM spend per evaluator so a runaway evaluator cannot blow your budget. LangSmith tracks evaluator spend in a rolling 7-day window and lets organization admins set a weekly cap on each evaluator's [attachments](/langsmith/evaluation-concepts#attachments), the projects and datasets the evaluator runs on. The cap can be a single organization-wide default or a custom override on a specific attachment.

LangSmith offers cost controls at three levels: [per-trace and per-model cost tracking](/langsmith/cost-tracking), [tracing usage limits](/langsmith/administration-overview#usage-limits), and weekly evaluator spend caps. This page covers the third.

<Warning>
Spend limits only enforce against runs whose model has [pricing configured](/langsmith/cost-tracking#create-a-new-or-modify-an-existing-model-price-entry) in LangSmith. Verify model pricing before relying on a limit, otherwise a runaway evaluator on an un-priced model will continue without enforcement.
</Warning>

<Note>
Spend totals are computed over a rolling 7-day window. The UI labels this window as **this week**.
</Note>

## How enforcement works

LangSmith records spend after each evaluator run completes, then sums spend across the rolling 7-day window. When the total reaches the effective limit, LangSmith pauses the evaluator on that attachment and resumes automatically once spend drops below the limit. In-flight runs may push the total slightly above the cap before they finalize, so an attachment can briefly overshoot by a small amount.

The agent and the trace are unaffected. Only the evaluator stops producing scores until spend drops below the limit.

## View evaluator spend

Spend appears in three places.

### Evaluators page dashboard

Navigate to the **Evaluators** page from the left sidebar. The top of the page shows a weekly view across the workspace:

- **Daily evaluator spend**: Stacked bar chart of spend per day. Toggle between **Evaluator** and **Project / Dataset** breakdowns.
- **Evaluator spend this week**: Total USD spend across all evaluators, with the change versus the previous week.
- **Evaluator runs this week**: Total run count across all evaluators, with the change versus the previous week.
- **Weekly evaluator spend limit monitoring**: Sorted list of top spenders with a per-attachment progress bar against its `$ spent / $ limit`. The header surfaces the count of attachments that have hit their limit (**Limit hit**) or are **on pace to hit limit**.

Use the **Prev week** and **Next week** controls in the page header to move the 7-day window.

The tracing project or dataset view has an **Evaluators** tab that mirrors these widgets scoped to that attachment, for example, **Daily evaluator spend on this tracing project**.

### Evaluators table

The Evaluators table on the same page includes:

- **Spend (this week)**: Total LLM cost for the evaluator across all attachments over the last 7 days. Sortable. Evaluators that do not call an LLM (for example, code evaluators) show no value.
- **Spend Status**: One of the following:
    - **Under limits**: At least one attachment has a limit, and none are at the cap.
    - **N limit hit**: One or more attachments have reached their limit and are paused. The number reflects how many attachments are paused.
    - **Unlimited**: No attachment has a limit set.

### Projects & Datasets tab on an evaluator

Open an evaluator and select the **Projects & Datasets** tab to see per-attachment spend and limits:

- **Spend (this week)**: Total LLM cost for the evaluator for that attachment over the last 7 days.
- **Current spend**: Progress bar showing spend against the limit over the rolling 7-day window.
- **Weekly limit**: Effective weekly limit for that attachment, either the organization default or a per-attachment override.

For attachment management, refer to [Manage evaluators](/langsmith/evaluators).

## Set an organization default spend limit

Organization admins set a single weekly cap that applies to every attachment across every workspace in the organization. There is one default per organization, not one per workspace.

<Note>Setting and editing the organization default requires the `organization:manage` [permission](/langsmith/rbac).</Note>

1. Open organization **Settings** and navigate to the evaluator spend limit entry under **Usage Configuration**.
1. Enter a USD amount. The unit is `/ week`. Leave blank for no limit.
1. Click **Save**.

If no organization default is set, attachments are unlimited unless a per-attachment override is configured. Clearing the default removes the cap from every attachment that currently inherits it.

Changing the default updates only attachments that inherit it. Per-attachment overrides are preserved.

## Override the default for an attachment

Organization admins can override the default for a specific attachment.

1. Navigate to **Evaluators** in the left sidebar and open the evaluator.
1. Click the **Edit evaluator** icon at the top right.
1. Under **Source**, select the specific attachment.
1. Scroll past **Filters** and **Sampling Rate**, then expand **Advanced**.
1. In the **Spend limit** field, set a custom USD amount. The unit is `/ week`.
1. **Save** the evaluator configuration.

The hint text below the field shows whether the current value is the organization default or a custom limit. To revert an override back to the organization default, click **Reset to organization default**.

Members without `organization:manage` see the limit but cannot change it. The read-only view shows one of:

- `Unlimited / week (organization default)`
- `$<amount> / week (organization default)`
- `$<amount> / week (custom limit)`

## When a limit is reached

When an attachment's weekly spend reaches its effective limit:

- LangSmith stops running the evaluator on new runs from that attachment.
- The Evaluators table **Spend Status** column shows **N limit hit**, and the Weekly evaluator spend limit monitoring widget surfaces the affected attachment.
- Skipped runs are not backfilled. Evaluation resumes automatically on new runs once the rolling 7-day spend falls below the limit.

## Configure model pricing

Spend tracking and limits depend on per-model pricing stored in LangSmith. Confirm that every model used by your evaluators has pricing configured in [Model pricing](/langsmith/cost-tracking#create-a-new-or-modify-an-existing-model-price-entry) and that runs surface cost via [Cost tracking](/langsmith/cost-tracking).

If a model has no pricing configured, its runs are not counted toward spend, and a spend limit cannot enforce against it.

## Troubleshooting

**Limit set but the evaluator still ran past it**: Check that the model used by the evaluator has a pricing entry in [Model pricing](/langsmith/cost-tracking#create-a-new-or-modify-an-existing-model-price-entry). Spend on un-priced models is not counted, and limits cannot enforce against zero spend.

**LangSmith spend does not match my LLM provider invoice**: LangSmith computes spend from the per-model rates configured in [Model pricing](/langsmith/cost-tracking#create-a-new-or-modify-an-existing-model-price-entry), not from your provider's billing. Differences are expected if your provider applies discounts, custom contracts, or model variants you have not added to LangSmith.

**Limit hit earlier than expected**: The window is a rolling 7-day window, not a calendar week. Spend from any run in the past 7 days counts, including spend from earlier in the previous calendar week.
